The Pearson correlation coefficient (r) measures the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two variables. It ranges from -1 to +1, where:

  • r = 1: Perfect positive correlation
  • r = -1: Perfect negative correlation
  • r = 0: No correlation

Correlation Analysis:

Land Area and Population


This analysis aims to determine if there is a significant correlation between the land area and population of 250 countries.

The hypothesis test uses a significance level of 0.05. If the calculated p-value is less than 0.05, the null hypothesis is rejected, indicating a significant correlation between area and population.

Additionally, the p-value indicates the probability of observing the data if the null hypothesis (no correlation) is true. A small p-value suggests that the observed correlation is unlikely to be due to random chance, providing evidence against the null hypothesis.


The null hypothesis (H0) in this hypothesis test is that there is no significant correlation between the area and population of the countries.
